# Agenda Cheat Sheet

## Day 01 — Understanding the shift

### 09:00 · Arrival & Coffee
- Soft start. Use it to greet, set the tone, and watch who clusters with whom.

### 09:30 · Opening Keynote — The Impact of Agentic Engineering
- **Pitch:** "We frame the shift, the gap between starting and transforming, and surface the open challenges this room is here to work."
- **Format:** Keynote, ~45 min, ends with the live mini-survey reveal.
- **Moderator note:** Keynote closes with the survey reveal, which is the bridge into the participant round. Don't break the momentum with admin announcements.

### 10:15 · Participant Round
- **Pitch:** "Now we get the room talking. Who is in this room, where are they on agentic maturity, and what is the question keeping them up at night."
- **Format:** 5 min on-stage survey reveal · 30 min table intros + discussion · 10 min nominee readouts.
- **Each person at the table answers three questions:**
  1. Who are you? (name, company, team size, role)
  2. Where are you with introducing Agentic Engineering to your org?
  3. What is your biggest open question this week?
- **Each table agrees on two things:**
  - **One headline** — the shared reality at this table, said in one sharp sentence.
  - **One hardest open question** — the one nobody at the table can confidently answer. Goes on the wall board.
- **Moderator note:** Pick a nominee per table. ~40 seconds on the mic, headline + question, no elaboration. Wall board stays up all day.

### 11:00 · Live Demo — Agentic Engineering in Action
- **Pitch:** "Fabian builds a real app live, end-to-end SDD with Codex Spark. The thing we ship today is the feedback tool we will actually use tomorrow."
- **Format:** Live build, narrated. Tobias co-narrates the *why* while Fabian types the *what*.
- **Moderator note:** Call out the steering moments out loud — "watch, Fabian is redirecting the agent right now, this is the moment most people miss." Track wall-clock counters on a slide so Fabian doesn't have to. Cover the silent stretches when the agent runs 30+ seconds.

### 12:00 · Lunch Break

### 13:00 · War Stories from CTOs
- **Pitch:** "Four CTOs tell the honest story of what actually happened when they tried to roll out agentic engineering. The bar: at least one thing they would do differently, said plainly."
- **Format:** 4 × (12 min story + 8 min Q&A) = 80 min · then 5 min synthesis.
- **Moderator note:** Open every Q&A with a pre-cooked hard question if the room is slow. Use pre-survey data to craft questions in real time. End with 2–3 cross-story patterns that seed the afternoon breakouts.

### 14:30 · Arthur Viegers @ Cursor — Your Software is About to Get a Mind of Its Own
- **Pitch:** "What if your software didn't just execute instructions, but made decisions, adapted in real time, and collaborated like a high-performing team member? Arthur takes us inside the world of agentic AI programming, then shows it live: code that writes itself, tests itself, deploys itself, fixes itself."
- **Format:** Keynote + live demo.
- **Moderator note:** Hand off cleanly. Don't soften the transition into "and now Arthur." Let his frame land.

### 15:30 · Breakout Sessions
- **Pitch:** "We surface what is hard, blocked, politically sensitive, unmeasured, or still unresolved. The output is a problem landscape the room recognizes as real."
- **Lead facilitator:** Fabian. **Floor facilitators:** Denis, Benedikt, Björn, Tereza, Tim, Fabian.
- **Six home tables, ~6–7 each.** Topics are drafts — adjust to the table's appetite.
- **Drafted topics (use or remix):**
  1. **Rollout and resistance** — Who is your most resistant engineer, and what is their real objection under the stated one? What part of the rollout have you tried and failed at?
  2. **Governance, compliance and procurement** — Single biggest approval/compliance/procurement blocker right now? What did legal, security, or procurement say no to that you still don't have a clean answer for?
  3. **Proof of value, metrics and budget** — What number are you using to justify adoption that you don't fully trust yourself? What should you probably be measuring but haven't started because it is hard, political, or ambiguous?
  4. **Org design, talent and management for 2027** — Which role, layer, or team boundary is already under pressure? If you hire fewer juniors, where do your future seniors come from?
- **Moderator note (facilitators):** Take notes — these feed Day 2's Solutions Workshop. Push past LinkedIn answers.

## Day 02 — Designing the future org

### 08:00 · Breakfast

### 09:00 · Solutions Workshop
- **Pitch:** "Yesterday we surfaced the challenges. Today we work the solutions. Each facilitator brings a topic shaped by what came up in the breakouts."
- **Format:** 10 min framing · 90 min deep dive in solution mode (facilitator takes notes on what was discussed and any agreed solutions) · 30–45 min table presentations and cross-room synthesis.
- **Moderator note:** Re-grouping is allowed if individual demand drives it. The notes become a handbook of challenges and solutions sent to all participants afterwards.

### 12:00 · Lunch Break

### 13:00 · Fireside Chat — Ernst-Cornelius Koch (Google) + Q&A
- **Who:** Head of Generative AI at Google.
- **Format:** Fireside chat + Q&A.
- **Moderator note:** Off the record where possible. Honest answers > polished ones.

### 14:00 · Peer Rotations
- **Pitch:** "Yesterday you mostly talked to your home table. Now we break that open: four rounds, four new people, nobody picks their own partner."
- **Format:** 2 min framing · 4 rounds × (10 min conversation + 5 min transition). Bell at each transition. Big screen shows the current round's full pairing list (Name A, Name B, table number).
- **Lead:** Fabian. Trainers float for reassignment.
- **Moderator note:** Pairings were pre-computed overnight using Day 1 data + pre-survey intent. Nobody sits out.

### 15:00 · Panel — The Future IT Organization
- **Pitch:** "By now the room has heard the frame, seen the tools, the peer stories, and spent two hours in breakouts. This panel pushes one level up: if implementation is no longer the bottleneck, what is the IT organization actually for?"
- **Format:** 5 min intro frame · 45 min moderated Q&A (5–6 pre-cooked questions, ~7 min each) · 10 min room Q&A.
- **Panelists:** 4, picked live during the day. Announced at the 15:30 coffee break the day before. Target mix: one listed/legacy voice, one hypergrowth scaleup, one small/services firm, one wildcard.
- **Core questions (pick 5 or 6 on the day):**
  1. If implementation is no longer the scarce resource, what is?
  2. In two years, your engineering headcount is higher, the same, or lower. Pick one.
  3. Name one role in your current org that will not exist in 2027.
  4. Feature teams of 2 to 4 people as the core unit. True for your org, or wrong?
  5. Painful migration vs. parallel reset. Which path are you actually on, and why didn't you pick the other one?
  6. Can you lead this transformation if you are not hands-on with coding agents yourself?
- **Moderator note:** Don't hide behind neutral moderation. Interrupt politely but firmly. 90-second cap on first answers, loosen in round two. Names not titles. If a panelist gives a LinkedIn answer, use contrast: "Lukasz, you run 15 engineers. Andreas, you run thousands. Is this really the same problem?" Off the record. No recording. No slides. Close with: "Dinner in 15 minutes. Keep arguing there."
